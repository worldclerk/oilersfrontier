Northwind Petroleums Limited · Calgary, AB, Canada

New Leadership Era at Northwind Petroleums: Michael Alexander Grant Steps In as Gunnar Lefebvre Retires

Outgoing Gunnar Lefebvre
Incoming Michael Alexander Grant
Effective Date June 22, 2026
New Role Exec. Chairman & COO/CFO

Northwind Petroleums Limited, a Calgary-based seller's mandate representing a Canadian petroleum refinery with over four decades of operational history, has announced a significant leadership transition at the executive level. Founder and longtime President Gunnar Lefebvre has officially retired, passing the reins to a seasoned global business leader with a career spanning more than three decades across energy, finance, and industrial operations.

Effective Monday, June 22, 2026, Michael Alexander Grant has assumed the position of Executive Chairman and Chief Operating & Financial Officer of Northwind Petroleums. The transition marks a new strategic chapter for the company, with Grant's multi-disciplinary background expected to strengthen its operational infrastructure, capital discipline, and global trade relationships in petroleum supply.

MG
Michael Alexander Grant
Executive Chairman & Chief Operating & Financial Officer — Northwind Petroleums Limited

Michael Alexander Grant is a global business leader with more than 30 years of experience spanning energy, agriculture, finance, infrastructure, and industrial operations. He is recognized for integrating operational excellence, financial discipline, and long-term strategic growth across multinational organizations.

In his current role, Grant oversees global operations, finance, strategy, capital allocation, risk management, procurement, and corporate development. He leads large-scale production networks across industrial and resource-based businesses, fostering a safety-first culture while driving reliability, efficiency, innovation, and sustainable growth.

Throughout his career, Grant has held progressively senior executive positions in the energy, agricultural, and industrial sectors. He has led large operating facilities, major capital projects, and complex business integrations while overseeing commercial, supply chain, finance, and operational functions. His experience includes responsibility for upstream operations, heavy oil and refining assets, manufacturing networks, treasury, investor relations, and corporate strategy.

Prior to this appointment, Grant served in several executive leadership roles including Chief Operating Officer, Chief Financial Officer, Chief Commercial Officer, and Chief Strategy Officer. He played a key role in multiple large-scale corporate transactions and business transformations, helping organizations improve operational performance while creating long-term shareholder value.

Grant also has extensive board and governance experience, serving on the boards of several industrial, infrastructure, and energy companies, and has chaired industry associations focused on sustainable resource development, financial stewardship, and corporate governance.

He holds a Bachelor of Science in Chemical Engineering, a Bachelor of Commerce in Finance, and a Bachelor of Arts in Economics and Sociology. He completed postgraduate studies in international business and Asian markets. Grant is widely respected for combining technical expertise, financial acumen, and operational leadership — with a philosophy that emphasizes safety, accountability, collaboration, and continuous improvement.
